Overview:
My project creates an online multiplayer game world simulation, a program designed to model 
how different regions in a virtual game world change over time. This software illustrates how players, 
non-player characters (NPCs), and in-game items change and interact across multiple regions as time passes.

Implementation:
I plan to use a std::map to store and manage data related to different regions in the game world. 
Each region will act as a key in the map, and the value will be a std::array containing three std::lists. 
These lists will represent players, NPCs, and items within that region.

One map entry would represent a forest region, where the three lists store all active players, 
NPCs (such as monsters or merchants), and items (such as weapons or potions) currently in that area. 
Another entry would be for a desert region, and another a snow region, each maintaining its own 
separate sets of entities.

Simulated events:
The simulation will focus on three events: player movement between regions; spawning and despawning of NPCs; and 
item generation or removal. At any given time period, players may travel to new regions, NPCs may appear or be defeated, 
and items may be created or consumed.
